Clean Transcript – DecisionAid & C&E Strategy Web App Review

Meeting: Bendigo Update
Date: 23 January 2026
Participants: Matt Griffin, Adam Riley, Rebecca Grant
Purpose:
	•	Review DecisionAid approach for Audit & Risk Committee
	•	Review current state of Communications & Engagement (C&E) Strategy Web App
	•	Identify gaps, usability issues, and future-direction features

⸻

1. Context: DecisionAid & ARC Presentation

The group discussed positioning the DecisionAid as an interactive, scenario-based tool rather than a static deck. The intent is to:
	•	Front-load councillors and ARC members with project benefits and context
	•	Allow ARC members to independently explore scenarios they may not raise in the meeting
	•	Shift focus away from single “headline numbers” toward levers, trade-offs, and control

There was strong alignment that:
	•	The tool should be sent ahead of ARC meetings
	•	ARC members are technical but not marketers — language must be clear, simple, and literal
	•	The tool must support both presentation mode and self-guided exploration

⸻

2. Strategy Web App – Overall Assessment

The C&E Strategy Web App was reviewed live during the meeting.

General reaction:
	•	The concept is highly valuable and strongly supported
	•	The idea of replacing static Word / Excel stakeholder documents with a living system resonated
	•	Participants described it as something they “would actually use in meetings”

The app is seen as the foundation for:
	•	Stakeholder tracking
	•	Action alignment
	•	Decision logging
	•	Future automated reporting (e.g. stakeholder consultation reports)

⸻

3. What Is Working Well

Conceptual Design
	•	The app reframes the C&E strategy as an operational system, not a document
	•	Stakeholder ledger, activity log, decision register, and actions are the right primitives
	•	Supports real-time use during meetings

Stakeholder View
	•	Ability to add stakeholders
	•	Clear intent to link stakeholders to:
	•	engagement history
	•	actions
	•	decisions
	•	Recognised as a major improvement over Excel-based tracking

Strategic Spine
	•	Having strategy, messaging, and narrative embedded is valuable
	•	Messages can (and should) evolve over time based on engagement

⸻

4. Current Functional Gaps Identified

The following issues were explicitly observed during live use:

Core Interaction Issues
	•	Some buttons and actions do not work consistently
	•	Adding actions sometimes fails
	•	Decision register and action log are not fully functional
	•	Items cannot reliably be deleted
	•	Editing existing items is inconsistent

Navigation & Usability
	•	No clear ability to scroll in some views
	•	Clicking a stakeholder does not consistently surface:
	•	engagement history
	•	outstanding actions
	•	next steps
	•	Mobile usability is limited

Data Relationships
	•	Stakeholders are not yet clearly linked to:
	•	past meetings
	•	decisions
	•	actions
	•	No consolidated “stakeholder history” view
	•	No clear ownership assignment (who owns which stakeholder)

⸻

5. Features Explicitly Requested or Implied

From discussion, the following capabilities were identified as high-value:

Stakeholder Management
	•	Clicking a stakeholder should show:
	•	full engagement history
	•	decisions involving them
	•	actions assigned
	•	next engagement or follow-up
	•	Ability to assign internal owner per stakeholder
	•	Ability to store basic contact details and roles

Logging & Automation
	•	Activity logs should:
	•	feed automatically into decision registers and action lists
	•	Decisions and actions should be:
	•	created once
	•	visible everywhere they are relevant
	•	System should support future one-click reporting:
	•	“Generate stakeholder consultation report”
	•	“Summarise engagement by stakeholder”

Workflow Intelligence (Future Direction)
	•	The system should eventually recognise stakeholder relationships (e.g. onion-ring model)
	•	When a key stakeholder is updated, the system should suggest:
	•	who else needs to be informed
	•	what follow-up actions may be required

⸻

6. Design Philosophy Reiterated

The group emphasised several guiding principles:
	•	Simple beats comprehensive — if it’s too complex, it won’t be used
	•	The app must be:
	•	fast
	•	intuitive
	•	forgiving
	•	This is a working tool, not a showcase
	•	Iteration is expected; perfection is not required immediately

There was strong agreement that:
	•	This app could become the equivalent of “Xero for stakeholder engagement”
	•	AI should reduce admin load, not add to it
	•	The system should feel like a command centre, not a form

⸻

7. Summary Direction for Next Iteration

The Strategy Web App should evolve toward:
	•	Fully interactive CRUD functionality (create, edit, delete everywhere)
	•	Clear linking between:
	•	stakeholders
	•	activities
	•	decisions
	•	actions
	•	Reliable usability across devices
	•	A UX that supports live meeting use without friction

The next build phase should focus on robustness, usability, and flow, not visual polish.
